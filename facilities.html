Research Facilities

State-of-the-art equipment for the synthesis, characterization, and device testing of low-dimensional quantum materials.

Synthesis & Fabrication

Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD)

Used for the high-quality, large-area growth of 2D transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDs) and van der Waals heterostructures.

Inert Gas Glovebox

Ensures an oxygen and moisture-free environment for handling sensitive nanoscale materials and assembling solar cell interfaces.

Material Characterization

Raman & PL Spectrometer

Crucial for determining the layer number, strain, and defect density of our synthesized 2D semiconductors.

Atomic Force Microscope (AFM)

Provides sub-nanometer topographical mapping to analyze the surface roughness and thickness of quantum material flakes.

Optoelectronic Testing

AAA Solar Simulator

Standardized illumination for accurately measuring the power conversion efficiency (PCE) of our hot carrier and interfacial solar cells.
